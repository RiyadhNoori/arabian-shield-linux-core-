2. Baseline Alignment / محاذاة خط الأساس

The red line marks the baseline. All letters should sit on or above it (descenders go below). If Arabic floats above the line, baseline is broken.

4. Positional Forms / الأشكال الموضعية للحروف

Arabic letters change shape depending on their position in the word (initial / medial / final / isolated). If shaping is broken, all letters appear in their isolated form and look disconnected.

Letter Isolated Initial Medial Final In a word
ب (ba) ب بـ ـبـ ـب كتب
ع (ain) ع عـ ـعـ ـع معلم
م (mim) م مـ ـمـ ـم قلم

5. Bidirectional Text / النص ثنائي الاتجاه

Each line should display in its natural direction. Check that numbers, punctuation, and Latin words appear in the correct position.

6. Diacritics / التشكيل

الْعَرَبِيَّةُ الْفُصْحَى لُغَةٌ جَمِيلَةٌ

Diacritical marks (Tashkeel) should appear precisely above/below their base letters. Displacement indicates a mark positioning bug.

Rendered with Amiri or Noto Naskh Arabic (serif style). Ligatures and diacritics should be properly formed.
